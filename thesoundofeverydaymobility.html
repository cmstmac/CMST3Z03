mdi-walkWhat is a Soundwalk?

A soundwalk is an exploration of the environment through listening. Participants move through a space, focusing on sounds both natural and human-made.

mdi-cellphone Stream 2: Record Sounds With Your Cellphone
Objective: Capture present-day sounds on campus to provide a current perspective.
  1. Choose a location on campus based on the theme you selected in Step 1, and head there (about 10 minutes)

  2. Using your phone's recorder, capture at least two to three different sounds related to your theme. Keep each recording short (30â€“60 seconds)

  3. Rename your files descriptively (e.g., library_path_footsteps).

  4. Tips for good recordings:
    • Hold your phone steady and close to the sound source.
    • Avoid covering the microphone.
    • Minimize background noise (wind, traffic, etc.).
    • Record multiple takes if needed.
    5. Example
    Record at the GO bus stop near McMaster. Capture the sounds of the bus arriving, doors opening, and passengers boarding. Pay attention to accessibility-related sounds, such as the ramp lowering or voice announcements. Reflect on how these sounds reveal challenges or supports for different kinds of mobility.

  5. Back in the classroom, transfer your recordings to your computer. (Options: email them to yourself, use AirDrop, Google Drive, or OneDrive)

  6. If your recordings are not already in .mp3 or .wav format, convert them using CloudConvert

mdi-walkStep 3: Test Your Soundwalk Draft (10 minutes)

Objective: Experience your soundwalk as a listener and take notes on what works and what could be improved.
  1. Use the QR code or link generated in the top menu to open your soundwalk on a mobile device.
  2. Go outside and walk through your soundwalk as if you were a participant, paying attention to the flow and clarity of echoes.
  3. Take notes on improvements: titles, descriptions, thematic connections, complexity/richness of sounds, and timing.
  4. Use your observations to inform the group soundwalk you will submit later this week.
