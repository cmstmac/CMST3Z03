mdi-walkWhat is a Soundwalk?

A soundwalk is an exploration of the environment through listening. Participants move through a space, focusing on sounds both natural and human-made.

This workshop will guide you through collecting and editing audio to give you ideas for your soundwalk recordings. By the end, you'll have hands-on experience with audio capture and editing techniques.


mdi-targetLearning Objectives

  • Identify and extract sounds from archival media.
  • Capture your own recordings of campus mobility.
  • Layer recordings with narration or text-to-speech.

mdi-toolsTools you'll use

  • Headphones or earbuds
  • YouTube, McMaster Films & Videos, McMaster YouTube channels
  • Your smartphone (iOS or Android)
  • Clipchamp (clipchamp.com)

mdi-lightbulbStep 1: Start with a Theme

Objective: Refine or reimagine your theme of mobility at McMaster. Your theme will guide your sound collection, editing, and connection to course readings.
  1. Revisit your proposal from last week. Remind yourself of the theme(s) you first imagined for your soundwalk. You can also see what other people are planning by reading the proposals on the discussion forum on A2L.

  2. Reflect on how your theme can connect with ideas from Weeks 1â€“4. For example:
    • How do different modes of mobility affect accessibility and inclusion on campus?
    • What sounds are associated with different bodies and spaces at McMaster?
    • Are there sounds or places that carry gendered, cultural, or social meanings?

  3. Write down a refined theme statement (1-2 sentences). Keep it flexible â€” your theme can evolve as you gather new sounds and ideas. If you haven't submitted a proposal yet, use this step to start your initial theme proposal.

mdi-microphoneStep 2: Collect Sound Recordings

Soundwalks can include many different types of recordings. For your project, you'll get to choose between using existing audio from archival footage or creating your own recordings with your cellphone (or even text-to-speech tools).


For today's workshop, start by exploring one of the sound collection streams below. If you finish one before class ends, try another! If you only get to one, that's fine too â€” you can always explore the others at home.


mdi-archive Stream 1: Collect Existing Audio Recordings
Objective: Identify and collect existing audio from archival footage reflecting mobility at McMaster.
  1. Browse the following sources for archival footage:
    • McMaster YouTube channel: https://www.youtube.com/@McMaster.U
    • McMaster Humanities channel: https://www.youtube.com/user/mcmasterhumanities
    • McMaster Films & Videos: https://library.mcmaster.ca/finding-aid/mac-films

  2. Identify a short video that connects with the theme you brainstormed in Step 1
    3. Example
    The following video shows how McMaster students reflect on gender roles in theatre: https://youtu.be/RNsN1xjZmss?si=Tpoz0oOz3NhuKXUT

  3. Download a short clip from the video using the tool https://ssvid.net/en

  4. Sign in to Clipchamp:
    • Go to https://app.clipchamp.com/login
    • Click "Work or School Account" and sign in with your McMaster email

  5. Create a new project:
    • Click Create Video Project
    • Give your project a descriptive name

  6. Import your video:
    • Click Import Media or drag your downloaded video file into the media library
    • Wait for the upload to complete

  7. Add video to timeline and extract audio:
    • Drag your video from the media library to the timeline at the bottom
    • Right-click on the video in the timeline
    • Select Detach Audio from the menu
    • Delete the video track by selecting it and pressing Delete (keep only the audio track)

  8. Edit your audio:
    • Trim the audio: Use the Slice tool (scissor icon) to cut unwanted portions
    • Add fade effects: Select your audio clip, then in the properties panel add Fade In and Fade Out effects
    • Adjust the duration of fades as needed (typically 0.5-2 seconds)

  9. Export your audio:
    • Click the Export button in the top right corner
    • Select More Options
    • Choose Audio-only
    • Name your file
    • Click export
    • Save your finished audio file to your computer
mdi-cellphone Stream 2: Record Sounds With Your Cellphone
Objective: Capture present-day sounds on campus to provide a current perspective.
  1. Choose a location on campus based on the theme you selected in Step 1, and head there (about 10 minutes)

  2. Using your phone's recorder, capture at least two to three different sounds related to your theme. Keep each recording short (30â€“60 seconds)

  3. Rename your files descriptively (e.g., library_path_footsteps).

  4. Tips for good recordings:
    • Hold your phone steady and close to the sound source.
    • Avoid covering the microphone.
    • Minimize background noise (wind, traffic, etc.).
    • Record multiple takes if needed.
    5. Example
    Record at the GO bus stop near McMaster. Capture the sounds of the bus arriving, doors opening, and passengers boarding. Pay attention to accessibility-related sounds, such as the ramp lowering or voice announcements. Reflect on how these sounds reveal challenges or supports for different kinds of mobility.

  5. Back in the classroom, transfer your recordings to your computer. (Options: email them to yourself, use AirDrop, Google Drive, or OneDrive)

  6. If your recordings are not already in .mp3 or .wav format, convert them using CloudConvert

  7. Sign in to Clipchamp:
    • Go to https://app.clipchamp.com/login
    • Click "Work or School Account" and sign in with your McMaster email

  8. Create a new project:
    • Click Create Video Project
    • Give your project a descriptive name

  9. Import your audio file:
    • Click Import Media or drag your audio file into the media library
    • Wait for the upload to complete

  10. Add audio file to timeline:
    • Drag your audio from the media library to the timeline at the bottom

  11. Edit your audio file:
    • Trim the audio: Use the Slice tool (scissor icon) to cut unwanted portions
    • Add fade effects: Select your audio clip, then in the properties panel add Fade In and Fade Out effects
    • Adjust the duration of fades as needed (typically 0.5-2 seconds)

  12. Export your audio:
    • Click the Export button in the top right corner
    • Select More Options
    • Choose Audio-only
    • Name your file
    • Click export
    • Save your finished audio file to your computer
mdi-account-voice Stream 3: Voice Annotations or Narrations
Objective: Create audio based on your own narration using text-to-speech voice in Clipchamp.
  1. Choose a location or space at McMaster that connects to your theme from Step 1.

  2. Write a short script (2â€“3 sentences) describing your experience, observations, or reflections on mobility in that space.
    • Your script could be creative (e.g., a poem or story) or factual (e.g., historical information or accessibility details).
    • Try linking your script to themes from the readings (e.g., accessibility, gender, migration, or mobile media art).
    3. Example
    Create a script about the McMaster Student Centre. You might describe its history as a student hub, the experience of navigating it as a student, or how it connects to broader ideas of accessibility and gathering.

  3. Generate an audio file using Clipchamp's Text-to-Speech:
    • Go to https://app.clipchamp.com/login and sign in with your McMaster email (Work or School Account)
    • Click Create Video Project
    • On the left panel, select Record & Create â†’ Text to Speech
    • Type your script into the text box and experiment with different voices and settings
    • Optional: Add background music or ambient sound to layer with your narration
      • On the left panel, select Music
      • Drag the desired music to the timeline

  4. Edit your music clip:
    • Trim the music clip: Use the Slice tool (scissor icon) to cut unwanted portions
    • Add fade effects: Select your music clip, then in the properties panel add Fade In and Fade Out effects
    • Adjust the duration of fades as needed (typically 0.5-2 seconds)

  5. Export your audio recording (voice plus music):
    • Click the Export button in the top right corner
    • Select More Options
    • Choose Audio-only
    • Name your file
    • Click export
    • Save your finished audio file to your computer
