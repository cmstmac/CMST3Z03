CMST 3Z03 - Mobile Practices, Technologies and Art

Group Project 1: The Sound of Everyday Mobility (35% total)

Working in pairs, you will create a soundwalk for a site or path at McMaster that reveals how people move through and experience that space. This assignment connects to topics from weeks 1-4.

What is a soundwalk? A soundwalk is a form of active listening that focuses on the acoustic environment of a specific location or route. Participants walk through a space while paying particular attention to the sounds around them. Learn more

Overall Learning Outcomes

By completing this project, students will:

  1. Create site-specific sound art: Develop technical proficiency in field recording, audio editing, and location-based technology (Echoes.xyz) to produce original artwork that reveals new perspectives on familiar spaces.
  2. Collaborate effectively in creative contexts: Work productively with partners to synthesize individual concepts into cohesive artistic work, while providing and integrating constructive peer feedback throughout the creative process.
  3. Connect artistic practice to theoretical frameworks: Analyze how sound-based art engages with concepts from mobile practices and technology studies, demonstrating understanding of how creative work can illuminate theoretical ideas about space, mobility, and everyday experience.
  4. Reflect critically on creative learning: Articulate creative decisions, evaluate different artistic approaches, and analyze personal development through the project process using clear written and oral communication.
